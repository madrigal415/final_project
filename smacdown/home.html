An Annual Mac and Cheese Cook-Off

What started out as a backyard BBQ turned into a yearly event bringing together the best macaroni and cheese dishes to battle it out for the much coveted Golden Noodle trophy while raising funds for Bay Area organizations. From sushi mac, to lobster mac, and even an ice cream mac, one thing has brought us all together: supporting our local community. Oh yeah, and a love for cheese.

The first event took place a few months after the devastating earthquake in Haiti, which claimed the lives of over 200,000 people. The event raised several hundreds of dollars for Partners in Health, the largest nongovernmental health provider in Haiti.

 Winning dishes for the inaugural event included a traditional mac and cheese baked in a cast iron skillet, a pesto mac, and a mac with fresh herbs.

“Raising hell in Dahlia Dell” was the theme of the second event in Golden Gate Park in San Francisco. Funds were raised for Bay Area food banks, which distributes 170.8 million pounds of food each year.

 Winning dishes included a fried mac and cheese balls, a four cheese mac dish, and a spicy mac.

This was the first year the event raised over $1,000 for W.O.M.A.N., Inc. (Women Organized to Make Abuse Nonexistent), an organization that has operated since 1978 as a community-based, multi-service agency, serving survivors of domestic violence in San Francisco and the larger Bay Area.

 Winning dishes included a waffle mac, a pork belly with szechuan spice mac, and a traditional three-cheese mac.

The fourth event was a packed backyard in San Francisco, and raised nearly $2,000 for Educators for Fair Consideration (E4FC), an organization empowering undocumented young people in their pursuit of college, career and citizenship. A few youth representatives were present and were able to share their stories with the crowd.

 Winning dishes included a five-cheese mac with jalapenos, a creamy mac, and a Welsh mac.

The fifth event raised over $1,000 for Mamacita’s Cafe, a mission driven social enterprise that prioritizes job creation and restorative leadership training for system’s impacted women ages 16-24 in Oakland, CA. Our mission is to create a safe space for young women to thrive through building economic opportunities, co-empowerment, leadership and job training. Mamacitas Cafe was founded by Directors Renee Geesler and Shana Lancaster who are fierce advocates of Oakland. They believe in cultivating long term economic opportunities for young women facing significant barriers to employment, with the goal of creating a space for incubation of entrepreneurial ideas to secure positive economic pathways for Oakland’s young women.

 Winning dishes included a brisket mac, a tikka masala mac, and a traditional, yet simple white cheddar mac.

The fifth year brought us back to the location of the first event in Berkeley, CA and raised over $1,000 for Beats, Rhymes, and Life a community-based organization that grew in response to a critical need for therapeutic programs designed specifically to serve boys and young men of color who as a group demonstrate some of the greatest health and social disparities. By implementing hip-hop therapy programs, BRL has shown that it’s possible to empower youth to direct their own care and recovery.

 Winning dishes included a crawfish mac, a mac dish with fried spinach balls, and a big shelled mac with 3 three cheeses..
